The Facts of the Human Brain

The human brain is the central organ of the nervous system. Along with the spinal cord, it comprises the central nervous system. It includes the cerebrum, brainstem, and cerebellum, and controls most bodily functions by integrating sensory input and coordinating responses.

Basic Facts of the Human Brain

  • The cerebrum is the largest part of the human brain.
  • The neocortex has six neuronal layers, while the allocortex has three or four.
  • The brain contains both neurons and supportive glial cells.
  • There are over 86 billion neurons in the human brain!

Cerebrum

  • The cerebrum is split into left and right hemispheres by the longitudinal fissure.
  • Asymmetry between the lobes is called petalia.
  • Each hemisphere is divided into four lobes:
    1. Frontal lobe
    2. Parietal lobe
    3. Temporal lobe
    4. Occipital lobe

Cerebellum

  • The cerebellum is a key feature of the hindbrain.
  • It plays a vital role in motor control and cognitive functions.
